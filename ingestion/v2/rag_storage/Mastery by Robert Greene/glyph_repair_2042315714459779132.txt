As he grew older, Mozart became increasingly independent and started composing his own pieces. His early works were mostly small-scale chamber music, such as sonatas for keyboard instruments, trios for strings and winds, and symphonies in the style of his favorite composer, Johann Sebastian Bach.

Mozart also began to explore other musical genres, including operas, which quickly became his favorite medium for self-expression. He composed numerous operas throughout his life, many of which are still performed today. His first major success as a composer came in 1786 with the premiere of his opera "Don Giovanni," which was an instant hit and is now considered one of the greatest works of art ever created.

In addition to his compositions, Mozart was also known for his incredible skill as a pianist. He often performed his own works at public concerts, dazzling audiences with his virtuosic technique and expressive playing. One of his most famous performances took place in 1784, when he gave a series of recitals in London. During these concerts, Mozart played some of his most challenging piano pieces, including the Piano Concerto No. 23 in A major, K. 488, which is widely regarded as one of the greatest works for solo piano ever written.

Throughout his life, Mozart remained fiercely devoted to music, constantly pushing himself to improve and expand his knowledge and skills.
